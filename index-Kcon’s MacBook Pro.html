Kyle Conniff

Kyle Conniff, Ph.D.

Statistician • Indigenous Health • Aging & Dementia Research

I am a statistician with an applied research focus in Indigenous health, aging, dementia, and research participation.

I am currently a postdoctoral trainee on the T32 Biology of Aging and Age-Related Diseases and a research associate at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I have a joint appointment in the Gleason Lab under Dr. Carey Gleason as well as in the Department of Statistics working with Dr. Rick Chappell.

I earned a Ph.D. in statistics (2024) at the University of California-Irvine (UCI). I worked under the direction of Dr. Dan Gillen in censored survival data with application to Native American health care. I received a Bachelor's of Arts from St. Norbert College (SNC) in mathematics in 2016.
