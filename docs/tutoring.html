▶ Rates and Pricing 💵
  • Online: $150/hour for high school and college‑level courses.
  • In‑person: I’m not actively tutoring in person. It’s possible, but the rate will be higher and must include travel time, travel costs, and the impact on my ability to schedule other students.
  • Children: Rates depend on subject matter and parental expectations. Creating lesson plans and teaching full subjects is more expensive than homework support. Please reach out so we can set an appropriate rate.

Note: All prices are negotiable. If you explain your situation, we can usually find a rate that works. Students paying for themselves typically have the most flexibility.

Quick questions: I don’t charge for short texted or emailed questions. If you have something quick, send a picture and I’ll reply in the same medium. If it requires substantial time, I’ll recommend a session.

▶ Tutoring Philosophy 🎓

My tutoring style is intentionally student‑centered. I rarely give answers outright, and I often turn questions back to the student. I encourage them to revisit their notes, break problems into smaller pieces, and connect new ideas to previously learned material. Tutoring, to me, is an opportunity to teach — just in a 1‑on‑1 format.

Tutoring also shapes how I design my teaching materials. Working with students has shown me which topics consistently cause difficulty and which explanations resonate. I build resources based on what benefits my tutees most. My goal is for every student to feel guided through the material as if they had a personal tutor — and tutoring is how I refine that approach.
